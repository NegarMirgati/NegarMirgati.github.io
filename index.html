About

I’m a computer scientist who cares about both sides of the work: the craft of building reliable, production-ready systems, and the curiosity it takes to wander into unknown territory.

My background spans backend development, machine learning research, and translating cutting-edge methods into things people can actually use. I’ve shipped Django APIs for healthcare workflows, taught game AI to undergraduates, and spent years in research labs studying procedural content generation, diffusion models, and how AI shows up in real products.

What ties it together is a belief that these aren’t opposing skills. They’re complementary ones. Shipping teaches you what matters in practice. Research teaches you what might matter next. I like working where those two conversations meet.

I love exploring new fields and working on very different projects. It’s my way of making every day different, fruitful, and memorable.
